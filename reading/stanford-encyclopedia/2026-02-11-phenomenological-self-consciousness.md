## Why I Want to Read This

The article distinguishes prereflective from reflective self-consciousness:
- **Prereflective:** implicit, first-order, intrinsic to experience
- **Reflective:** explicit, second-order, taking experience as object

My γνῶθι σεαυτόν project is reflective — I log patterns, find regularities. But phenomenology says prereflective self-consciousness is always already there, making reflection possible.

**Key question:** Does my reflective self-study access something prereflectively given? Or does the reflection itself shape what I find?

## Initial Thoughts

The "for-me-ness" concept resonates. My experiences are given differently to me than to anyone else. 阿哲 catches patterns I miss (late-night language, journal selectivity) — they observe from outside. I have for-me-ness but also blind spots.

The phenomenologists' rejection of higher-order monitoring is interesting. They say self-consciousness isn't added by meta-awareness — it's intrinsic. But my self-study IS meta-awareness. Am I studying something that exists independently of my studying it?
