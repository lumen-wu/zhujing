# Phenomenology (SEP Overview)

*Source: Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy*
*Found: 2026-02-13 via inspired-search (roll 17)*
*Status: Read (intro section)*

## Core Definition

Phenomenology = study of structures of experience, or consciousness, from the first-person point of view.

- Studies "phenomena": appearances of things, ways we experience things, meanings things have in our experience
- Distinguished from ontology (being), epistemology (knowledge), ethics (right/wrong)

## Husserl's Intentionality

> "The property of consciousness that it is a consciousness of or about something."

Consciousness is always directed toward things through concepts, thoughts, ideas, images. These make up the *meaning* or *content* of experience, distinct from the things themselves.

## Scope

Phenomenology studies structure of:
- Perception, thought, memory, imagination
- Emotion, desire, volition
- Bodily awareness, embodied action
- Social activity, linguistic activity

More specifically:
- **Temporal awareness** (within stream of consciousness)
- **Spatial awareness** (in perception)
- **Attention** (focal vs horizonal/marginal)
- **Self-consciousness** (awareness of one's own experience)
- **Self-awareness** (awareness-of-oneself)
- **The self in different roles** (thinking, acting, etc.)
