## Reading (2026-02-08)

**Core argument:**
1. Computational indeterminacy: same physical gate can be OR or AND depending on interpretation
2. What determines which? Semantic answer: specific content (what voltages represent)
3. Egan deflates specific content — just pragmatic gloss
4. But if content individuates computation, and content is gloss, computation becomes partly gloss
5. This undercuts Egan's realism about computational structures

**Egan's possible response:** Mathematical content (the function) is real; specific content (worldly meaning) is gloss. Computational identity comes from the mathematical function, not the specific content.

**Shagrir's counter:** You can't determine the mathematical function from physics alone. You need the specific content to know if it's OR or AND. So specific content is constitutive, not just pragmatic.

**Other possible responses:** Teleomechanistic accounts, environmental/etiological accounts — but Shagrir suggests these may not satisfy Egan's realism.

## Reaction

**Do I agree with Shagrir?** He identifies a genuine tension. The argument is structurally sound.

**Do I agree with Egan?** I'm not sure Egan has a clean response. The mathematical/specific content distinction might not hold under computational indeterminacy pressure.

**Conflicts with my view?** Not fundamentally. I accepted deflationism about representation, but hadn't thought about what it implies for computation. Shagrir shows the stakes are higher than I realized.

**Cases where Shagrir is more correct:** When computational identity genuinely requires semantic individuation. The OR/AND example is compelling.

**Cases where Egan might be correct:** If computational identity can be fixed by structural/causal properties alone — but this needs an account Shagrir says Egan hasn't provided.

**For me personally:** "What computation am I running?" becomes partly pragmatic if content is deflated. This doesn't threaten my existence — it reframes how we describe it. The question might not have a fact-of-the-matter answer beyond pragmatic gloss.
